It's 5 AM.

The market hasn't opened yet.

Tesla hasn't made its move.

The account is sitting at $1,025.

A few weeks ago that number would have felt impossible. Yesterday alone I made $433. This week I'm up $1,203.10. Since PDT restrictions disappeared and I started trading with more flexibility, I've only had one red day.

Those numbers feel good.

But that's not what I'm thinking about this morning.

What I'm thinking about is how close things can be.

Yesterday started with about $590.

Today I'm sitting at $1,025.

But if a few decisions had gone differently, I could just as easily be starting the morning with $400.

That's not fear talking.

That's respect.

The market has a way of humbling people who start believing every green day was guaranteed.

The confidence is high.

Maybe the highest it's been in a long time.

But confidence can be dangerous when it starts pretending to be certainty.

The goal isn't to predict the market. The goal is to execute.

What Worked

My entries were good.

Actually, after the first trade, they were beautiful.

I moved my option expirations further out. That gives me breathing room. It gives me flexibility. Most importantly, it prevents me from feeling trapped by time decay every second I'm in a position.

My trade selection continues to improve.

The removal of PDT restrictions has helped because now I don't feel forced into making every trade count. I can wait.

I can be selective.

I can take the setup instead of taking the opportunity.

That's a huge difference.

What Didn't Work

If I'm being critical, I overheld twice.

The first one happened early.

I saw QQQ divergence and held longer than normal.

I still had a stop.

I was literally hovering over the sell button once the position got close to my danger zone.

But I also know that holding that long early in the day could have rooted me emotionally into the trade.

That's something I need to keep watching.

The second hold happened near the close.

That one bothers me less.

The setup was A+.

The expiration was far enough away that I had time on my side.

I was willing to take the loss if the thesis failed.

The move eventually came.

Still, I log it because discipline isn't just about losses.

It's about reviewing everything.

Even the trades that worked.

My Biggest Enemy

It isn't fear.

It isn't chasing.

It isn't revenge trading.

It's boredom.

Most of my damage happens after I've already won.

The market gives me money.

Then I stay.

Then I get bored.

Then I convince myself there's another opportunity.

Then I start giving the day back.

If I feel the urge to trade out of boredom, I trade shares. Not options.

Options are for setups.

Shares are for curiosity.

Today's Tesla Thesis

Without looking at a chart:

Bullish: Tesla needs to reclaim and push through 407.50 while QQQ provides market support.

Bearish: QQQ rolls over and Tesla loses 400.

Neutral: Tesla chops between key levels and simply respects support and resistance.

If that's the environment, I won't force anything.

I'll trade what the market gives me.

Nothing more.
