New Year, Same Lesson — My First L of 2026

FreeRideInvestor — TBOW Tactics Journal

First trading day of the year… and I’m staring at an L that feels louder than it should.

Not because losses are new. They’re part of the game. But because it’s January 1st energy — the day you’re supposed to feel clean, reset, locked in. And instead I’m watching my account bleed like it’s trying to remind me who I am when discipline disappears.

The hard part isn’t that I lost. The hard part is I knew what I was supposed to do.

What happened

I had calls. I had a plan. I even had the levels. Reclaim zones. Targets. A clear “sell strength” moment.

And then I did what I always do when I’m tired of “missing the full move”… I let hope take the wheel.

That’s the addiction right there: the desire to catch the entire move. Not the smart slice. Not the high-probability portion. The whole thing — top to bottom — like the market owes it to me because I saw it early.

Today punished that mindset. Not with a gentle slap. With a straight drop that made the P&L scream.

The real enemy: discipline debt

People think trading is charts. It’s not. It’s rules.

And every time I break a rule, I take on discipline debt. At first it looks small — “just one more candle.” But the interest rate is brutal. It compounds into bigger position sizes, worse entries, weaker exits, and a brain that starts negotiating with reality.

The part that hurts the most? This wasn’t just “trading money.” This was bill money.

And on the first day of the year, that fact hits different. It makes me sit here and ask myself: Do I really want to keep doing this to myself?

What I’m taking from this

  • One contract means one decision. If I want “full move” potential, I need structure (2 contracts or a spread), not vibes.
  • Targets are not suggestions. If price hits the zone, I get paid. Period.
  • No more bill-money trades. If it can’t be lost, it can’t be traded.
  • Exits are my edge. I’m not losing because I can’t read charts. I’m losing because I refuse to obey them.

What happens next

I’m not posting this to be dramatic. I’m posting it because this is the line.

If I’m building FreeRideInvestor into something real — something repeatable — then I can’t keep running a strategy powered by emotion and sleep deprivation.

The market will always be here. But my time, my energy, my peace… that’s not infinite.

So here’s the commitment, written in public: 2026 is the year I stop confusing hope with a plan. TBOW is permission-based. If permission is gone, I’m gone.

Disclaimer: This is my personal trading journal, not financial advice. I’m documenting decisions, emotions, and process — because the only way I win long-term is by becoming the kind of trader who follows his own rules.
